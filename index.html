Explaination

"Term Frequency — Inverse Document Frequency" is abbreviated as TF-IDF. It's a statistical method for determining the importance of a word in a document based on how frequently it appears in that document and a set of documents (corpus).


The idea behind this metric is that if a word appears frequently in a document, it must be more important and relevant than words that appear less frequently, so we should give it a high score (TF). However, if a word appears frequently in a document but also in a large number of other documents, it is likely not a relevant or meaningful word, and we should give it a lower score (IDF). The amount of information a word provides about its context is proportional to its relevance (a sentence, a document or a full dataset). The more relevant words enable us to comprehend the entire document without having to read it in its entirety
